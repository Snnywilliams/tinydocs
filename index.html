TINY DOCS

We believe in creating a healthier and happier planet. Welcome to Tiny Docs—a Chicago based health and wellness website designed to help kids and families understand surgical procedures and health & wellness issues. Our animated short films, staring Dr. Patches and Nurse Scrubs, will help kids and families prepare for and understand issues related to health.

Our Story


The team at Tiny Docs has a simple mission - to create a healthier, happier planet. We know the medical world can be intimidating, especially for children, so we’re providing a solution to that problem, with a long-term global focus on educating children across the world on health and wellness. Tiny Docs is a web platform that provides resources to educate kids and families about health issues and surgical procedures without the 'doctor-ese'. Our site offers short cartoons featuring our stars, Dr. Patches, a precocious 10-year-old girl physician and her sidekick, Nurse Scrubs, an 8-year-old boy wonder nurse. Our library of cartoons will educate kids (and parents) about a wide range of health issues in an entertaining and educational way.

Nice Words

"Children often worry about things that happen when they go to see the doctor. They worry about pain, or being separated from their parents, or just the unknown. Children today enjoy using games to learn. So one idea with great potential to help allay fears about health care is to use electronic platforms that gamify and cartoonify information to speak to children in a language they enjoy.”

- Dr. Mary Ciccarelli, Associate Professor of Clinical Medicine and Pediatrics, Indiana University Medical Group

"Helping kids and their families understand what to expect with their medical care is extremely important for increasing confidence in the treatment provider and participation in their care, and making them more likely to follow through with recommendations. When patients know what to expect, they can give more accurate and complete information about themselves and their symptoms, enhancing their care overall."

- Dr. Nneka E. Onyezia, PhD, ABPP, NorthShore Medical Group

"As a nurse, when I heard about Tiny Docs, I knew it was something I had to be apart of. Tiny Docs offers a way for children to understand illness or procedures that their medical team may not be able to communicate. By adding a fun story for the child and family to watch together, questions can be answered and worries can be reduced. What is even better, Tiny Docs can act as an inspiration for children to engage in their care and be enthused about staying healthy."

- Laura Ritchie, RN, Rush University Medical Center
